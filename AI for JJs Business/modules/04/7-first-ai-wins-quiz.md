lessonType: quiz
questions:
  - prompt: What makes a strong first AI pilot use case?
    options:
      - Narrow scope, clear owner, measurable KPI, low operational risk
      - Broad enterprise transformation across all functions
      - Safety-critical automation with no manual fallback
      - Undefined goals so teams can explore freely
    answer: Narrow scope, clear owner, measurable KPI, low operational risk
    explanation: Small, controlled pilots are easiest to validate and improve.
  - prompt: Which KPI is most useful in an early pilot?
    options:
      - Time saved per task or cycle
      - Number of tools purchased
      - Total prompt count regardless of quality
      - Amount of documentation generated
    answer: Time saved per task or cycle
    explanation: Early pilots should prove practical value with operational metrics.
  - prompt: What should happen after a successful pilot?
    options:
      - Document learnings, refine controls, then scale deliberately
      - Immediately roll out unchanged to every team
      - Remove governance checks to keep momentum
      - Start a new pilot without closing the first one
    answer: Document learnings, refine controls, then scale deliberately
    explanation: Controlled scaling prevents rework and governance gaps.
