questions:
  - prompt: What is the strongest foundation for a future-proof AI roadmap?
    options:
      - A phased plan with capability uplift, governance, and measurable outcomes
      - A one-off tool purchase with no follow-up plan
      - Immediate enterprise-wide automation regardless of readiness
      - Vendor-driven roadmap without internal ownership
    answer: A phased plan with capability uplift, governance, and measurable outcomes
    explanation: Future-proofing needs staged delivery and clear ownership.
  - prompt: Which action best supports ongoing team capability?
    options:
      - Build regular learning loops and practical role-based enablement
      - Rely on one champion only
      - Freeze all process changes after first rollout
      - Avoid documenting lessons learned
    answer: Build regular learning loops and practical role-based enablement
    explanation: Capability grows through repeated practical training and reflection.
  - prompt: What indicates an AI initiative is sustainable long-term?
    options:
      - Demonstrated value, managed risk, and clear operational ownership
      - High novelty with no measured business impact
      - Tool usage growth regardless of quality
      - Expanding scope faster than governance can keep up
    answer: Demonstrated value, managed risk, and clear operational ownership
    explanation: Sustainability depends on value, controls, and accountable operations.
